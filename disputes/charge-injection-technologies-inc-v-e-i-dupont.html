Champerty & maintenance

Charge Injection Technologies, Inc. v. E.I. DuPont

Delaware Superior Court · US (DE) · 2016 · Decided

Citation/Docket: 2016 WL 937400 (Del. Super. Mar. 9, 2016)

Parties & Funders

Posture: Defendant attacked plaintiff's funding as champertous
Funder(s) involved: Burford Capital

Background

DuPont argued CIT's funding agreement with Burford was champertous/maintenance and stripped CIT of real-party-in-interest status.

Holding & Outcome

Rejected — funder had no control of the litigation and no assignment of the claim; agreement lawful in Delaware.

Practical Lesson

The control/assignment line is the key champerty firewall in commercial funding: passive, non-controlling capital survives.
