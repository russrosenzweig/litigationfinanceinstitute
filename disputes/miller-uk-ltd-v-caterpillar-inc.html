Privilege & work product

Miller UK Ltd. v. Caterpillar, Inc.

U.S. District Court, N.D. Ill. · US (IL) · 2014 · Decided

Citation/Docket: 17 F. Supp. 3d 711 (N.D. Ill. 2014)

Parties & Funders

Posture: Defendant sought funding documents in discovery
Funder(s) involved: (Funder identity confidential)

Background

Caterpillar sought Miller's litigation funding documents, arguing champerty and relevance. The leading early U.S. opinion on funding discovery.

Holding & Outcome

Court rejected the champerty attack and held materials shared with funders under NDA retained work-product protection, though the funding agreement itself was not privileged as a communication.

Practical Lesson

Use NDAs and common-interest frameworks before sharing evaluative materials with funders; assume the agreement itself may be discoverable even where deal communications are protected.
