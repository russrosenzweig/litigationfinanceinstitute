The Fortress–Therium Realignment

In June 2025, Fortress Investment Group quietly assumed operational control of Therium Capital Management's global litigation funding portfolio, in what is widely regarded as the largest realignment in the litigation finance asset class since the UK's PACCAR decision tightened recoverability rules for funders. The transfer covered more than forty active disputes spanning Europe, Asia, and North America, following months of restructuring at Therium, one of the industry's longer-established UK-founded funders.

Fortress, an alternative-assets manager overseeing roughly $70 billion, had already built meaningful exposure to legal assets — around $6.8 billion — and had deployed more than $4 billion into litigation finance since forming its dedicated legal finance arm in 2013. Under the new arrangement, Fortress sets budgets, determines strategy, and authorizes capital calls for every matter that previously sat under Therium's management, reportedly with a materially different operating style: quarterly performance metrics and weekly reporting requirements, replacing Therium's historically more flexible, claimant-friendly approach to drawdowns and timelines.

For claimants and law firms, the episode is a reminder that a funder's identity at the time an agreement is signed is not guaranteed to be the funder actually managing the matter years later — and that the ongoing wave of regulatory tightening (PACCAR in the UK, new state disclosure laws in the US) is accelerating consolidation among funders themselves, not just among the claims they finance.
